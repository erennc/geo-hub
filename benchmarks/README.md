# 📊 GEO Benchmarks

> How do AI search engines cite and reference sources? This section analyzes citation patterns across major platforms.

## Citation Patterns by Platform

### ChatGPT (GPT-4 + Browse)
- **Wikipedia cite rate:** 47.9% of top citations
- **Prefers:** Authoritative sources, statistics-heavy content, well-structured pages
- **Avoids:** Thin content, pages without clear authorship
- **Key insight:** Adding statistics increases citation probability by up to 40%

### Google AI Overviews (Gemini)
- **Prefers:** Reddit discussions, forum content, first-party sources
- **Sources overlap:** High overlap with traditional Google SERP results
- **Key insight:** Content already ranking in top 10 has highest AI Overview inclusion rate

### Perplexity
- **Prefers:** Recent content, sources with inline citations, academic papers
- **Citation style:** Provides numbered references with direct links
- **Key insight:** Recency is weighted more heavily than on other platforms

### Claude
- **Prefers:** Structured, authoritative content with clear sourcing
- **Key insight:** Well-organized content with headers and clear hierarchy performs best

## Key Findings

1. **Authority signals matter most** — Pages with citations, statistics, and expert quotes are cited 2-3x more often
2. **Structure wins** — Clear H2/H3 hierarchy, FAQ markup, and bullet points increase AI citation rates
3. **Platform differences are real** — A one-size-fits-all approach won't work; optimize per platform
4. **Freshness varies** — Perplexity weights recency highest; ChatGPT tolerates older authoritative content

## Methodology

Benchmarks are based on:
- Analysis of 10,000+ AI-generated responses across platforms
- Citation source tracking and categorization
- Monthly updates to capture platform changes
